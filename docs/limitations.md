# Limitations

- Six recordings are insufficient to demonstrate clinical or population-level generalization.
- The default single-recording test set is useful for engineering verification, not definitive benchmarking.
- White Gaussian noise does not represent all real environments.
- Noisy-phase reconstruction limits achievable quality.
- Objective metrics do not replace respiratory-domain or perceptual expert review.
- Speech and respiratory sounds may have different spectral priorities; future work should report them separately.
- The predecessor result of −9.3226 dB output SNR is documented as an unsuccessful prototype result, not promoted as evidence.
- The repository is research software, not a diagnostic or monitoring device.
